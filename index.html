What Is Obesity?

Obesity is a life-long progressive, life-threatening, genetically-related, costly, multi-factorial disease of excess fat storage

Obesity Defined

Obesity is defined by having a BMI greater than 30, or by having a fat percentage of 24% or above for men and 35% or above for women.

*The term obesity means we are now at very high risk of having serious health problems like high blood pressure, high cholesterol or diabetes due to our excess weight.

Morbid Obesity Defined

Morbid obesity generally refers to when a person is 100 lbs. or more above their ideal weight, or when their BMI is greater than 40.

*Morbid obesity means that we are statistically less likely to live as long as we should due to our excess weight.

Obesity Is a
Big Problem

55% American adults are overweight.

25% of American children are overweight.

  • Obesity is a major public health problem worldwide affecting 25% of industrialized world.
  • Obesity alone costs America 6% of health care expenditures - $238 billion / year.
  • Approximately $30 billion is spent annually in the pursuit of weight loss.
  • 400,000 deaths per year are due from obesity ALONE, not from the diabetes, heart disease and increased cancer risk caused by obesity.
